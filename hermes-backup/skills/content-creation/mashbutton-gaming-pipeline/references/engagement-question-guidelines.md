# Engagement Question Guidelines for MashButtonGaming Shorts

## Core Principle
The closing engagement question is the **primary comment driver**. It must target a specific, debatable aspect of the game/news — not a generic binary or yes/no prompt.

## Question Design Patterns

### 1. Mechanic-Focused (Highest Performance)
Target the signature/new mechanic that defines the game:
- "Is the world-reshaping mechanic exciting or just a gimmick?" (HAEX)
- "Will the alien seed terraforming add depth or become a chore?" (HAEX fallback)
- "Does the DMZ extraction loop work in a PvE chapter?" (Tarkov Icebreaker)

### 2. Pedigree vs. Genre Pivot
Target the tension between developer history and new direction:
- "Would you trust Division devs with survival horror, or is the world-reshaping mechanic just a gimmick?" (HAEX — combined mechanic + pedigree)
- "Can the Battlefield portal team make extraction shooters work?" (Arc Raiders)

### 3. Player Tension / Forced Choice
Target a genuine player dilemma or frustration:
- "Forced upgrade or justified evolution?" (Warzone EOS on last-gen)
- "Worth migrating to current-gen for Warzone, or time to quit?" (Warzone EOS alternative)
- "Buy MW4 day one or wait for Season 1 Warzone integration?" (MW4 reveal)

### 4. Design Trade-off
Target a specific design decision with clear pros/cons:
- "Cross-crew progression: brilliant flexibility or progression dilution?" (HAEX shared progression)
- "Separate character/world saves: replayability or fragmentation?" (HAEX save system)

### 5. Market Timing / Viability
Target community skepticism about business model or timing:
- "Early Access 2027 — too late for the survival hype cycle?" (HAEX)
- "Free-to-play extraction shooter in 2026 — saturated or opportunity?" (Arc Raiders)

## Anti-Patterns (Reject These)

| Anti-Pattern | Example | Why It Fails |
|--------------|---------|--------------|
| Binary good/bad | "Survival genius or just Division 2.0?" | No nuance, drives "good" / "bad" comments only |
| Generic hype | "Are you hyped?" | Zero substance, low-quality engagement |
| Yes/No factual | "Will you play this?" | Binary, no discussion |
| Vague speculation | "What do you think will happen?" | Too open, no anchor for debate |
| Multi-topic | "Mechanic, graphics, and release date thoughts?" | Diluted focus, scattered comments |

## Validation Checklist
Before submitting a narration with an engagement question:
- [ ] Targets ONE specific mechanic, design choice, or player tension
- [ ] Has no "correct" answer — reasonable people disagree
- [ ] Invites substantive reasoning, not one-word replies
- [ ] References something concrete from the narration body
- [ ] Uses "or" construction to frame the debate explicitly
- [ ] Ends with `?` (mandatory)

## Word Count Note
The engagement question is part of the 50–100 word narration budget. Keep the question concise (~15–20 words max) so the factual body has room.
